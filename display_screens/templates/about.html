What is This and Why Does it Exist?

As someone who is both interested in and trades volatility in microcap stocks, I built this project as a supplement to my work. To be profitable with any consistency you have to understand what kind of market you are in. Is the market hot or cold relative to what it was in the past? If things are hot are there any patterns in what’s running? Is there a particular theme or industry that’s getting a lot of attention right now? Is there a catalyst pushing that attention? Knowing the answers to these questions can mean the difference between a string of big winners or taking consecutive losses. The easy(ish) money is made in an active market. Unnecessary losses happen when things are slow. Think of it in terms of fishing. You can have the best bait, gear, and technique in the world but if there’s no fish you aren’t catching anything. Likewise, even with a bad strategy, it’s infinitely easier to be profitable in a market with the right conditions. The problem with this is the amount of time and energy needed to figure out if the status quo is even worth risking money for. Trends change, often quickly. If you take a week off its entirely possible you’ll come back to a market that’s unrecognizable from what it was when you left. Then you have to go through the process of getting reacquainted with the new state of affairs. This project is my attempt at mitigating that re-learning curve and to provide a better perspective once you’re caught up.


In a nutshell, this project gets, cleans, saves, and displays historical stock screens for the top percent gainers of the day as well as various metrics relevant to the companies found in those screens. The general purpose is to gauge how active this market niche is relative to how active it’s been in the past as well as provide some insight into the characteristics of companies that are/were seeing large intraday price swings. Specifically, this alleviates the following pain points:


First, the data isn’t easy to come by for free. Gainer screens for the current day are easy to get but scans from days/weeks ago all exist behind a paywall. This produces historical data for free.


Second, the most extreme levels of intraday volatility are almost exclusive to seedy microcap stocks - stocks that nobody cares about until they blow up. Because of this, information for these companies can be (and usually is) scarce across mainstream sources. Details such as a stock’s float or short interest, are extremely important when assessing the quality of a potential trade. Since this site uses many different sources for screens, company stats, and price data, the results are more likely to be complete.


Third, even if money is exchanged for historical screen data, not only is there no guarantee I’m getting the information I want, it’s also going to be a chore to extract the insights I want from it. By being in control of what’s collected and where it’s collected from, I can produce better data and create my own custom views to answer whatever questions I have.


Fourth, as previously mentioned, volatility is cyclical. Sometimes it’s there, sometimes it’s not. Unless you’re at the desk every day keeping tabs on things you won’t really have an accurate idea of how excited the market is from one point in time to the next. Without that reference you can’t really have any idea if now is a good time to be involved. With this you can simply look at a chart.
